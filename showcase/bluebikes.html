Points farming is real — and detectable

76 suspicious bidirectional pairs flagged (Isolation Forest + rules, contamination swept 0.05–0.20). Top pair 41↔9: 11,002 trips, avg 2.3 min, 0.4 km, 98% members.

The farm is in Kendall Square

  • #1 points route: Ames St at Main St → MIT Stata — 240 m apart, station surplus +20,451 vs deficit −9,217.
  • All top farming routes sit in the Kendall corridor — where Bluebikes pays points to rebalance.
  • Extreme case: pair M32070 ↔ M32071 — repeated 55-second, 100 m flips.
  • Top pair 41↔9 activity stops in March 2023 — worth checking against valet / points-program changes.

Dock strategy: move steel to demand

Expand

  • Ames St at Main St — 19 docks serving ~120K trips
  • MIT Vassar St
  • Kenmore Square

Reduce

  • Mt. Hope St
  • Newton Highlands
  • Centre St, West Roxbury
  • Kendall is the system's biggest commuter sink (AM/PM ratios: MIT Stata 0.10, Ames St 0.11) — chronic afternoon overload.
  • Caveat: ranking used a stale station list — re-verify against live GBFS before committing capital.

Broken docks are invisible in trip data — so we log GBFS

13
stations with docks disabled >48 h in the first monitoring pass
22
stations with ≥1 disabled dock right now
2
fully offline: Marion St at Harvard St · Cummins Hwy at Blue Hill Ave
  • A dead dock generates no trips — you cannot see it in ride history at all; the logger snapshots every station every 10 minutes.
  • Detection rule: stuck full/empty while ≥2 neighbors within 500 m keep churning, or num_docks_disabled > 0 persisting over 48 h.
  • Flags are field-check candidates from a first pass, not confirmed outages — the list sharpens as the log grows.
