The premise: metadata is enough

  • Input: public trip records only — start/end station, timestamp, bike type, member flag. No GPS traces, no rider IDs.
  • Fused with open feeds: GBFS station status, NOAA weather, ACS census, MBTA v3 alerts, OSRM street routing.
  • Output: fleet health, rider rejections, land use, equity flows, fraud, and causal demand effects — everything in this deck.

The signal source: 8 years of trips

Sep 2018 – Jun 2026. Strong seasonality, COVID shock, sustained growth — plus messy realities handled in the pipeline: schema break in 2024 (bike IDs dropped), station-ID migration in 2023, negative-duration rows filtered.

Inference #1 — fleet health from behavior

Rejection signature: 60–120 s trip, then another departure from the same station ≤5 min (41.3% of short trips). A behavioral health score over 6,891 bikes correlates with rejections at r = −0.28 (p < 0.0001) → ~58 degrading bikes/month, ≈ $70K/yr repair exposure — from metadata alone.

Inference #2 — land use from timestamps

  • One feature — the AM/PM departure ratio — separates residential from employment zones with no land-use data at all.
  • Residential extreme: TD Garden–West End Park, ratio 130 : 1.
  • Employment extremes: UMass Boston 0.07, MIT Stata 0.10, Ames St 0.11.
  • Kendall Square emerges as the system's biggest commuter sink; network clustering finds 54 Louvain communities (modularity 0.617).

Inference #3 — de-biasing haversine speeds

Straight-line speeds systematically understate reality. Measured street circuity = 1.19 (trip-weighted; median 1.155) by routing the top-60 station pairs through OSRM. Corrected speeds: e-bikes 15.0 km/h vs classic 11.1 (+35%); members 12.9 vs casuals 10.4. Elevation contributes <5% — Boston is flat.

Inference #4 — equity flows from OD matrices

  • Station-to-station flows mapped to census block groups → Moro segregation index (SI) with density-product weights.
  • Permutation testing with 8 controls separates signal from geography: income SI 0.758 loses significance under density controls (p 0.41 → 1.0); race SI 0.440 survives all of them (p = 0.0).
  • Balanced 243-station panel (via a station-ID crosswalk) shows the apparent SI decline is network expansion, not behavior change.
  • Natural experiment: Dec 2023 e-bike launch shifts the race-SI trend (ITS, p = 2.6×10⁻⁷).

Method detail: composition bias is the trap

The full-system trend and the fixed-panel trend tell opposite stories. Any longitudinal mobility metric on a growing network needs a balanced panel — here that required a crosswalk across the 2023 numeric→alphanumeric station-ID migration.

Method detail: weather-controlled effects

  • Question: do transit disruptions push riders to bikes?
  • Poisson GLM over 1,516 days: naive −2.5% → weather-controlled −2.2%. Small, negative, stable → bikes and the T are complements.
  • Weather is the real driver: temp r = +0.66; adding weather lifts demand-model R² from 0.80 to 0.90.
  • Effect size is spec-sensitive (continuous measure: −0.41% → −0.26%) — we report both.

  • The framing is the method: this does not say the network is misplaced. Actual siting optimizes trip demand + capacity redundancy (downtown clustering), not residential coverage — the number quantifies that trade-off.
  • Without the random control, +30.2pp reads as an indictment; with it, most of the headroom is just where people live.

Anomaly flags need a behavioral second stage

Stage 1 (Isolation Forest, contamination swept 0.05–0.20) flagged 56 unique "farming-like" pairs. Stage 2 — tidality (AM/PM directional flow) + flip-session detection (3+ alternating rides within minutes) — reclassified them: 15 tidal last-mile commute corridors, 41 mixed local hops, 0 farming. Bonus catch: the top pair's apparent March-2023 stop was a station-ID schema artifact; crosswalked, it's a 20,188-trip BU commute corridor. Anomaly ≠ abuse — the metadata can tell the difference.

A GBFS logger snapshots station status every 10 minutes — first pass already flagged 13 stations with docks disabled >48 h and 2 fully offline; broken docks are invisible in trip data alone.

Why this maps to StreetLight's problems

  • Portable by construction: every method needs only GBFS-style metadata + open covariates — it transfers to any bikeshare, scooter, or CDR-like OD dataset. NYC replication is scoped next.
  • Metadata-only equity metrics (SI + permutation controls + balanced panels) — a product-shaped answer to agencies' equity reporting mandates.
  • Fleet health & anomaly scoring as derived data products on top of operator feeds.
  • Calibration assets: measured circuity (1.19) and weather-controlled baselines de-bias speed and demand estimates at scale.
